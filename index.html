Stay Safe Online: Standing Strong Against Cyberbullying

Helping young people build safe, kind, and respectful online spaces.

Group Members: Add your names here

Cyberbullying is one of the biggest challenges young people face in the digital world. Unlike face-to-face bullying, it can happen anytime, anywhere. This site will help you understand what cyberbullying is, why it matters, and how to protect yourself and others.

Why Cyberbullying Matters

Cyberbullying affects millions of young people worldwide. Unlike traditional bullying, it follows people into their homes through phones, tablets, and computers. The impact can be severe ‚Äî leading to stress, anxiety, lack of sleep, and even depression.

  • Fact 1: 1 in 5 Australian young people have experienced cyberbullying (eSafety Commissioner, 2022).
  • Fact 2: Victims are 1.9x more likely to experience low self-esteem and poor academic performance (eSafety Commissioner, 2023).

Ask the Wellbeing Wiz!

Q1: I keep getting mean comments on Instagram posts. Should I delete my account?

A: No need to delete your account! Block the people sending the comments, report the posts, and talk to a trusted adult. Remember, bullies want a reaction ‚Äî not giving them one is powerful.

Q2: My friend is getting bullied in a group chat. What should I do?

A: Your support matters! Privately message your friend, check on them, and if safe, speak up in the group chat. Encourage them to block and report the bully.

Q3: In online games, I get nasty messages. How can I stop this?

A: Use mute, block, and report tools. Play with friends when possible. Screenshot messages and tell a trusted adult if it continues.
