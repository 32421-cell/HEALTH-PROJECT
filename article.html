Why Cyberbullying Matters

Cyberbullying is bullying that happens online or through mobile devices. It may include mean messages, spreading rumours, exclusion, or sharing private images without consent. Because it happens online, it can be persistent and can be seen by many people quickly.

For young people, this can cause anxiety, sleep problems, lower school performance and sadness. It's important to take online harassment seriously and know how to respond.

1 in 5 Australian young people reported being cyberbullied (eSafety & surveys).
1.9x Higher chance of low self-esteem for victims (studies & reports).
